Alternative Dispute Resolution

The development of alternative dispute resolution (“ADR”) stemmed from the need for reasonable allocation of judicial resources.  Its main purpose is to reduce the number of litigation cases submitted to the court and allow the parties to resort to a non-litigation approach for dispute resolution and save costs.  However, modern types of disputes tend to involve various professional areas (such as construction disputes and financial products disputes with banks, insurance companies and securities firms), so there is an increasing appetite from the parties to resort to professionals outside the courtroom to step in and resolve the disputes.  Thus, the Code of Civil Procedure, the Arbitration Law, the Financial Consumer Protection Act, the Township Mediation Rules, etc. have set forth different ADRs.  How to select the most advantageous and cost-efficient approach and formulate the most appropriate strategy and guidelines under a specific ADR ultimately lies in the advice of a professional lawyer.
